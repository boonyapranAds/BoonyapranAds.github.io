Losing Round

Roles Director · Editor
Genre Psychological Drama
Status Festival Submissions
01 / 05 Losing Round
Watch on YouTube →

Phum, a young boxer, grew up shadowed by the silent weight of his father Chai's unspoken defeat. He believes that one victory in the ring will finally earn him his father's love. But the deepest fight is the one inside his head.

In the world of boxing, the winner is strong and successful. The loser is weak. For Phum, losing means worthlessness and failure in his father's eyes. As crowds watch the fights for entertainment, Phum battles a fear no amount of training can defeat. He must learn to face that fear, and to accept himself without needing anyone else's approval.

— Director's Statement

This film is about facing the problems we hide inside, alone. The protagonist's greatest enemy is his own fear — fear of failure, fear of not being loved. These fears take over without him realising, and even victory cannot drive them out. It only changes their shape. What he has to learn is to face them.

In a time when social media demands constant self-exposure while pressuring us to hide what we truly feel, many people will recognise themselves in this character. Anxiety and mental health struggles can happen to anyone, but our society still treats these issues as taboo. Some people don't even recognise what they're going through. I wanted to show how serious these struggles can be, and how heavy they feel from the inside.

Boxing is the central metaphor — a sport defined by strength and combat, the opposite of the fragile mind it conceals. To step into the ring is to step into the fight inside yourself.

I want the audience to hear the voices in Phum's head, to feel the weight of what he carries, and to face those fears alongside him.

A Story of Korn & Kaew

Roles Director · Writer · Producer · Editor
Genre Romantic Drama
Format Short Film
01 / 05 A Story of Korn & Kaew
Watch full film → Watch trailer →

A prequel to Methin The Musical, exploring the relationship between two close friends caught between friendship and love.

This film explores the relationship between Korn and Kaew, two close friends caught between friendship and love. As Kaew remains emotionally closed off, Korn must decide whether to hide his feelings or risk everything by revealing them. The film was made to give audiences understanding of the characters before seeing the live stage production in London.

I led the project through writing, directing, producing, and editing, collaborating with my cinematographer. Although the story followed narrative guidelines from the musical, I had the freedom to creatively shape the tone and visual storytelling. We developed and completed the entire project within two weeks, from scripting to the final edit.

This project pushed me to improve my direction and collaboration skills not only with actors but also across departments. The experience of shooting outside in the heart of London was unforgettable — we faced many challenges, especially around location permission, but with quick thinking and problem solving we adapted and made it work.

Magnum Opus

Roles Director · Scriptwriter
Genre Comedy Thriller
Recognition 11 Awards · UAL Festival
01 / 05 Magnum Opus
Watch full film →

A dark comedy thriller about a detective interrogating a mentally unstable artist suspected of murder — only to uncover a chilling twist that she may be his next victim.

I created the original concept, wrote the script, and directed the film. I led the pre-production process, collaborating with my team to craft storyboards, floor plans, lighting plans and shot lists, which helped streamline production. I also worked closely with actors, directing them through tailored direction techniques and scenario-building to bring out authentic performances.

This project taught me the critical value of pre-production and how nuanced directing can be, especially when it comes to communicating my vision to actors. We faced many challenges, from limited time for story development to issues with lighting and set design. However, we shifted the genre, adding the comedic tone which made the film more engaging and sharpened its message.

Magnum Opus was the most challenging and stressful project I've worked on, but also the one I learned the most from.

Hero

Roles Producer · Director · Cinematographer · Editor
Format Short Documentary
Note First Solo Project
01 / 05 Hero
Watch full film →

A short documentary exploring the passion and dreams of an underground music producer, revealing the drive behind his creative journey.

This was my first solo project as a filmmaker, handling everything from concept development and storyboarding to lighting, filming, and editing. It was also my first experience with the documentary form, where the narrative was shaped through interviews rather than a script.

I learned how to operate a camera by myself and create a three-point lighting setup using household lamps and soft light. I also discovered how important post-production storytelling is in documentary filmmaking — the right questions can shape the story.

What made this project special was how the subject's passion for music mirrored my own passion for filmmaking. This remains one of the projects I'm most proud of — not only because I handled everything myself, but for its independence and authenticity.

Full Moon

Roles Director · Scriptwriter · Editor
Genre Romance
Mentor Boonsong Nakphoo (DOP)
01 / 05 Full Moon

— Short Film

Inspired by a Haruki Murakami short story.

A poetic short film inspired by a Haruki Murakami story, exploring themes of loneliness, emotional emptiness, and the misguided search for love as a cure.

The concept suggests that true fulfilment comes not from others, but from within yourself. This was my first short film, created under the mentorship of acclaimed Thai director Boonsong Nakphoo, who also served as cinematographer. With his guidance, I learned the filmmaking process from the ground up — developing ideas from scratch, scriptwriting, acting (to better direct actors), storyboarding, shooting, and editing.

This was a life-changing project. Making Full Moon while learning the art of filmmaking showed me how powerful and important film can be as a tool of communication. It not only taught me the foundations of filmmaking but also affirmed my decision to pursue this path as a career. Despite a long and challenging shoot, it remains my most personal and honest work.

Chungking Express — Recreation

Role Director
Format Shot-for-Shot Recreation (1 minute)
Original Wong Kar-wai (1994)
Watch on YouTube →

A shot-for-shot recreation inspired by Wong Kar-wai's Chungking Express, aiming to replicate the film's vivid colour palette, stylised cinematography, and emotional tone of loneliness.

As director, I focused on precisely matching camera movement and actor timing to the original sequence. I worked closely with the team to recreate lighting setups and framing, using the original film as a visual guide.

This project developed my understanding of cinematography and visual storytelling. It also sharpened my attention to detail as I worked to replicate a master filmmaker's style. The film received multiple awards at a UAL first-year film showcase.

— Stage · 2025

Methin The Musical

Roles Associate Director · Writer · Music Producer
Format Original Stage Production (Thai)
Audience ~300 · London
01 / 05 Methin The Musical

— Stage Production

Premiered London, 2025. Six-month production from concept to stage.

A murder mystery musical following Korn — a hardworking young man entangled in class divides, murder, and deception — and his lover Kaew, who may herself be a suspect.

I co-directed the production, wrote the full-length script (over 80 pages), and composed original music pieces. I also oversaw key creative decisions, from casting and rehearsals to photoshoots and visual direction. The show sold about 300 tickets and received enthusiastic feedback from the audience.

This was my first experience in stage production, where I learned how theatre differs from film, particularly in acting style and visual language. Writing a complex murder mystery was challenging — the case had to be engaging enough to draw the audience into the investigation, build suspense without giving too much away, and deliver a final reveal that felt like the last jigsaw piece of the puzzle.

The six months of production were challenging and high pressure, but the experience strengthened my skills in directing, scriptwriting, collaboration, and creative problem-solving.
